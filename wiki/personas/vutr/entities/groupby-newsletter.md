GroupBy is Vu Trinh's own weekly link-roundup newsletter — the "SELECT *" byline shows up in the earliest captured issues (#1–#3, September 2023), and by issue #6 he introduces himself consistently as "Vu Trinh, a data engineer currently working at a mobile game company" who reads "good stuff (related to data and engineering)" and shares it. From issue #11 onward the self-introduction drops the "mobile game company" detail and simply reads "a data engineer" — the posts themselves never explain why, so it isn't safe to assume a job change from this material alone.

The format visibly evolves across the batch. Issues #1–#3 are short and unstructured: three to six links, each given a one- or two-line description in Vu's own words, occasionally paired with an image lifted from the source article. By issue #6, GroupBy has settled into a fixed set of recurring, emoji-labeled sections that persist through #25: "🚀/⚙ Engineering," "✏ Data" (opened by a line from the film *Predestination*), "🤖 AI┆ML┆Data Science" (opened by a line from *Aliens*), and "🔥/💣 Catch up with the world" (opened by a line from *Back to the Future*) for terse product/release announcements. The "🚀/⚙ Engineering" section's epigraph itself changes partway through: issues #6 and #7 open it with a Wikipedia-sourced definition of engineering ("Engineering is the practice of using natural science, mathematics, and the engineering design process..."), but from issue #11 onward it opens instead with a line from the film *Memento* ("I have to believe in a world outside my own mind."), and it's this Memento line — not the Wikipedia definition — that stays stable through #25. Every issue in that stretch signs off with the same *Terminator 2* line, "Hasta la vista, baby." From issue #21 a "📈 Career" section is added at the top, and issues #21–#25 append a "💠 Previously on Dimension" cross-promotion box for a sister newsletter called Dimension, plus (in #24–#25) a referral-reward program with referral-count milestones — rewards scale with "the number of friends you refer," not with subscriber counts. Issue #36 drops the movie-quote framing and the fixed section emoji altogether in favor of a flatter list of links, each tagged with an estimated reading time and topic labels (e.g. "15 mins, Agoda Engineering — Topics: Kafka, data infrastructure, software engineering").

Within each issue, the substantive content is almost entirely curatorial: a linked external post (an engineering blog from Meta, Uber, Netflix, LinkedIn, Discord, Agoda, PayPal, Lyft, Grab, DoorDash, or a named independent writer such as Zach Wilson, Chad Sanderson, or Chris Riccomini) accompanied by a short quoted excerpt from that source and, occasionally, one added sentence of Vu's own framing (e.g. "The correct question might be 'Why is MapReduce slower than Spark?'" in #3, or "Can say that while Spark has become a more popular choice... the same has happened with cloud storage" also in #3). The technical claims inside the linked articles belong to their original authors, not to Vu, and this wiki does not attribute them to him. What is genuinely his own voice in this batch is the newsletter's editorial apparatus itself (section structure, recurring quotes, self-introduction, referral mechanics) and the occasional first-person aside — most substantially the [[pet-project-retention-and-collaborative-practice|pet-project reflection]] in issue #36, and a shorter production note in issue #12 where he describes moving from "bookmarking cool stuff" and "manually compiling it" to "(70%) automatically compiling it using Python."
