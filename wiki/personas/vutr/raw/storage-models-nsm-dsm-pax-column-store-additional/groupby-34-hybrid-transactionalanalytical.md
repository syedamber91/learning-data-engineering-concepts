## ✏️ My curated list

#### 💠┆**[Hybrid Transactional/Analytical Storage](https://jack-vanlightly.com/blog/2024/5/2/hybrid-transactional-analytical-storage)**

* *10 mins, by Jack Vanlightly*
* *Topics: Table Format || Stream Processing || Object Storage*

> *In this article, the author will discuss the [Confluent](https://www.confluent.io/lp/confluent-kafka/?utm_medium=sem&utm_source=google&utm_campaign=ch.sem_br.brand_tp.prs_tgt.confluent-brand_mt.xct_rgn.apac_lng.eng_dv.all_con.confluent-general&utm_term=confluent&creative=&device=c&placement=&gad_source=1&gclid=Cj0KCQjwudexBhDKARIsAI-GWYXuvfmdzgadSITuAqyhrVM4eIBe8Uv6ubKbzouxcDOlszq4SPyDBskaAhfPEALw_wcB)’s vision has for using object storage (with the two recent announcement of [Freight Clusters](https://www.confluent.io/blog/introducing-confluent-cloud-freight-clusters/) and [Tableflow](https://www.confluent.io/es-es/blog/introducing-tableflow/)) as an enabler of a new storage paradigm - Hybrid Transactional/Analytical Storage.*

#### 💠┆[LakeChime: A Data Trigger Service for Modern Data Lakes](https://www.linkedin.com/blog/engineering/data-management/lakechime-a-data-trigger-service-for-modern-data-lakes)

* *15 mins, by Linkedin Engineering Blog*
* *Topics: Data Lake || Table Format || Data Pipeline*

> *In this blog post, we introduce LakeChime, a data trigger service that unifies data trigger semantics not only among modern table formats, but also between modern and traditional table formats such as Hive, bridging the impedance mismatch between traditional partition semantics and modern snapshot semantics.*

#### 💠┆[From ETL and ELT to Reverse ETL](https://luminousmen.com/post/from-etl-and-elt-to-reverse-etl)

* *5 mins, by luminousmen*
* *Topics: Data Pipeline*

> *In recent years, we've witnessed a significant transformation in data management, moving from the traditional **ETL** (Extract, Transform, Load) framework to the more agile **ELT** (Extract, Load, Transform) methodology. However, an even newer trend, Reverse ETL, is reshaping our approach to data integration.*

#### 💠┆[From Predictive to Generative – How Michelangelo Accelerates Uber’s AI Journey](https://www.uber.com/en-SG/blog/from-predictive-to-generative-ai/)

* *20 mins, by Kai Wang, Min Cai, Joseph Wang and Eric Chen from Uber*
* *Topics: AI || Machine Learning*

> *As Uber’s centralized ML platform, [Michelangelo](https://www.uber.com/blog/michelangelo-machine-learning-platform/) has been instrumental in driving its ML evolution since its introduction in 2016. In this blog post, we will see how Michelangelo empowers many end-to-end high-quality ML applications at Uber.*

#### 💠┆[Ensuring data reliability and observability in risk systems](https://engineering.grab.com/data-observability)

* *5 mins, by Yi Ni Ong, Kamesh Chandran and Jia Long Loh from Grab*
* *Topics: Data Management*

> *As Grab’s business grows, so does the amount of data. Any data discrepancy or missing data surely impact the business operation. They need to quickly detect any data anomalies, which is where data observability comes in.*

#### 💠┆[The only two log levels you need are INFO and ERROR](https://ntietz.com/blog/the-only-two-log-levels-you-need-are-info-and-error/)

* *8 mins, by Nicole Tietz-Sokolskaya*
* *Topics: Programming || Software Development*

> *It's common for us to log in ways that are unhelpful.*

#### 💠┆**[Database in Kubernetes: Is that a good idea?](https://medium.com/@fengruohang/database-in-kubernetes-is-that-a-good-idea-daf5775b5c1f)**

* *13 mins, by Vonng*
* *Topics: Database || Infrastructure*

> *Whether databases should be housed in Kubernetes/Docker remains highly controversial. While Kubernetes (k8s) excels in managing stateless applications, it has fundamental drawbacks with stateful services, especially databases like PostgreSQL and MySQL.*

#### 💠┆**[The Foundation of Data Validation](https://towardsdatascience.com/the-foundation-of-data-validation-0163892e5aa1)**

* *7 mins , by Chengzhi Zhao*
* *Topics: Data Processing*

> *Discussing the basic principles and methodology of data validation*

#### 💠┆**[Modeling Slowly Changing Dimensions](https://medium.com/towards-data-science/slowly-changing-dimensions-6a08dc0386ae)**

* *16 mins, by Giorgos Myrianthous*
* *Topics: Data Modeling*

> *A deep dive into the various SCD types and how they can be implemented in Data Warehouses*

#### 💠┆[How an empty S3 bucket can make your AWS bill explode](https://medium.com/@maciej.pocwierz/how-an-empty-s3-bucket-can-make-your-aws-bill-explode-934a383cb8b1)

* *4 mins, by Maciej Pocwierz*
* *Topics: Cloud || Object Storage*

> *Imagine you create an empty, private AWS S3 bucket in a region of your preference. What will your AWS bill be the next morning? Two days later, I checked my AWS billing page, primarily to make sure that what I was doing was well within the free-tier limits; My bill was over $1,300, with the billing console showing nearly 100,000,000 S3 PUT requests executed within just one day!*
