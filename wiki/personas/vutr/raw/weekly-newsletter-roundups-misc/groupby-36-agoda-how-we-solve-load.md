## **😊 It’s Vu Trinh here …**

> *Sharing*

Working with pet projects is a good way to learn data engineering.

There were times I loved finding new pet projects on the internet (like a collector)

I opened the browser and entered something like “data engineering side project.”

I picked the one that excites me the most.

Spending all weekend following the tutorial and finally getting things up and running.

I feel proud of myself. I feel like I have accomplished something.

But two weeks later, I remember nothing. Empty head….

…and you know what? I started looking for different projects to play with.

The loop continues on and on, and I barely learn anything from those projects I’ve done.

I know that many data engineers try to learn by doing as many pet projects as possible, but you will learn nothing just by getting things up and running.

Here are my notes when doing a pet project.

* Set expectations on what you will learn.
* Do things right: don’t just try to get things done, for example

  + Learn the fundamentals and best practices of Docker, Git,…
  + Write and organize code nicely, write tests,…
  + The order of SQL execution,…
  + …
* Try to do the project with your friends. This will give you a good impression of how to work on a project with more than one person.
* When playing with any tools (Spark, Airflow, Git, Docker…), ask, “What problem do these tools try to solve?” and “How are they gonna solve that problem?”
* After doing the project, try to put yourself in the role of a user who needs to consume the data from the data warehouse or the dashboard you’ve just set up, and ask yourself, “Am I satisfied?” “Do I get things I need?”. Because, as a data engineer, most of the time, you will work to serve the internal-data-consumed user
* Apply data modeling to your project.

So, what is your lesson after doing the pet project?
